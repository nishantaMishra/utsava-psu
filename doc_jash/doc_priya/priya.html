मित्र-प्रिया से संवाद

Mitra-priyā se Saṃvāda 

 मित्र-प्रिया से संवाद 

											छन्द – चौपायी
बहा ले गयी मित्र हमारा । उसकी तेरी सम्मति धारा ।।
सुखिता उसकी थी तुमसे ही । अपर मानता था हमसे भी ।।
जागा सहसा विराग कैसे । त्याग गयी गौतम के जैसे ।।
निष्ठुर क्यों ठाना आचार । न आवाहन सन्देशाचार ।।
जब थी वह पूरा तेरा ही । तू गयी रहा ना मेरा भी ।।
तापवन्त है उसका चेतस् । व्यकुलाता रजनी को बहुशस् ।।
उसकी देख दीनता गाढ़ी । फटती आज हमारी छाती ।।
असुख मिटा ओ उसकी जारा । लौटा हमको मीत हमारा ।।  
जन्या की वह सन्तत माला । छिन्न भयी सूनी उरशाला ।।
विपुल राग उसपर दिखलाती । क्यों पर अब परिहार जताती ।।
रति सलिला थी तुम कल्याणी । नित्य भाव पोखर को पानी ।।
मोड़ चली पथ जलज नसाये। चित्त कूल में विदर बनाये ।।
कातर नयन बरसते जल से । मर जाए ना हृदय भङ्ग से ।। 
आजीवन विद्वेष पालते । उभय पक्ष विच्छेद हारते ।।
प्रतिगमन करो अब टेक त्याग । बान्धव का लेकर सुहास ।।
विनय हमारा सुनले वामा । सखा प्राण में पुन मुस्काना ।।

										~ निशान्त मिश्रा
										

 Context 

The poet is consumed by sorrow, not for himself, but for his dearest friend, who has been shattered by love’s cruel departure. His friend’s beloved vanished without a word, leaving behind only silence and heartache. In the wake of this abandonment, the friend was no longer the man he once was; grief had reshaped him into a shadow of his former self. The poet, feeling as though he had lost his friend to despair, resolves to seek out the lost beloved. With hope as his guide, he sets forth to speak with her, believing that if he can persuade her to return, he may not only mend his friend’s broken heart but also restore the bond they once shared.


 Translation 

बहा ले गयी मित्र हमारा । उसकी तेरी सम्मति धारा ।।
सुखिता उसकी थी तुमसे ही । अपर मानता था हमसे भी ।।
The current of love that flowed between you and him carried my friend away, just as a river sweeps away all in its path. To him, you were always above all else—even above me.
जागा सहसा विराग कैसे । त्याग गयी गौतम के जैसे ।।
निष्ठुर क्यों ठाना आचार । न आवाहन सन्देशाचार ।।
Whence did this sudden aversion take root in your heart, so fierce and unyielding that you abandoned him as Gautama once did? [The Buddha, in his silent retreat from worldly ties, left his companion without a word, and so too the sage Gautama, in tales of old, parted from his beloved forever.] Why, then, do you act with such merciless resolve—offering neither the solace of a voice call nor the fleeting comfort of a written message?
जब थी वह पूरा तेरा ही । तू गयी रहा ना मेरा भी ।।
तापवन्त है उसका चेतस् । व्यकुलाता रजनी को बहुशस् ।।
When you were in his life, he belonged entirely to you. But since you left, he is no longer even mine. His mind is tormented by unbearable anguish, and restless nights have become his constant companion.
उसकी देख दीनता गाढ़ी । फटती आज हमारी छाती ।।
असुख मिटा ओ उसकी जारा । लौटा हमको मीत हमारा ।।  
Seeing his deep despair, my heart feels as if it will burst. O his lover, erase his sorrow and return our beloved friend to us.
जन्या की वह सन्तत माला । छिन्न भयी सूनी उरशाला ।।
विपुल राग उसपर दिखलाती । क्यों पर अब परिहार जताती ।।
The sacred garland of love that once adorned his heart now lies broken, leaving his soul desolate. You once showered him with boundless affection—why, then, do you now turn away in abandonment?
रति सलिला थी तुम कल्याणी । नित्य भाव पोखर को पानी ।।
मोड़ चली पथ जलज नसाये। चित्त कूल में विदर बनाये ।।
O gracious one, you were once the flowing river of love, the ever-present water that nourished the lake of his heart. But now, you have turned away, drying up its depths, leaving the lotuses withered and his Citta(the conscious brain) fractured with sorrow
कातर नयन बरसते जल से । मर जाए ना हृदय भङ्ग से ।। 
आजीवन विद्वेष पालते । उभय पक्ष विच्छेद हारते ।।
His weary eyes weep endless tears, fearing his end due to heart-shatter. If resentment is nurtured for a lifetime, both hearts will be lost, and only separation will triumph.
प्रतिगमन करो अब टेक त्याग । बान्धव का लेकर सुहास ।।
विनय हमारा सुनले वामा । सखा प्राण में पुन मुस्काना ।।
Abandon your resolve and return once more, bringing with you the warmth of his smile. Hear my humble plea, O beloved—let joy return to the soul of my dearest friend.
